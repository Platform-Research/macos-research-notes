# Research Principles

## Evidence First

Every claim should be tied to one of:

- A source file or artifact.
- A command output.
- A log.
- A public reference.
- A reproducible experiment.

Label anything else as inference or speculation.

## Small Notes Win

Prefer many focused notes over one giant document. A good note explains one class, kext, framework, symbol group, boot phase, or experiment.

## Repeatability Beats Drama

Do not optimize for impressive targets first. Optimize for workflows that can be repeated across macOS releases.

## AI Is a Research Assistant

Use AI to summarize, compare, plan, generate boilerplate, and inspect patterns. Do not let AI invent facts about private Apple internals without evidence.

## Publish With Humility

Use careful language:

- "Observed" for direct evidence.
- "Suggests" for supported inference.
- "Unknown" when the data is not enough.
- "Speculative" when exploring a hypothesis.
